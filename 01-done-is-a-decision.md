# Done Is a Decision

*The two moments, the bracket and the six false forms of done*

## Done is not a property of the artifact

No artifact contains its own completion. A memo does not know whether it answered the question. A model does not know whether its output is fit for the use the Human intends, because fitness lives in the intended use, the affected parties and the consequence of error, none of which are in the text.

Before AI, this truth was hidden by friction. Production was slow, so the maker's accumulating effort served as a rough gauge: after three drafts and two reviews, the work was probably close. The gauge was never accurate, but it was correlated. AI removes the correlation. Generation is instant, tireless and always fluent, so every output arrives wearing the costume of finished work. The costume is not a lie exactly. It is a default. Text generators produce text that looks the way finished text looks. What has changed is not that unfinished work exists but that unfinished work is now indistinguishable from finished work by inspection of surface alone.

So done must be decided. And a decision requires a standard, a decider and a record.

## The two moments

**Moment one: Define.** Before work begins and before AI touches the task, the Human translates the task into a small set of observable pass or fail tests. The Slow AI Kitchen fixes the form at Gate 1: three to seven tests covering substance, usability, constraints and sign-off, abbreviated to one to three tests for Tier 1 work. The form is not the hard part. The hard part is that the tests must be anchored to consequence rather than to effort or appearance, which requires the Human to know, before any output exists, what the work must do in the world and what it must never do. That knowledge is the substance of Informed Intent at task level.

**Moment two: Confirm.** After the work is built and verified, a named Human decides that the definition is met and releases the work within a defined scope. Confirmation is not review. Review inspects the work product. Confirmation attaches the Human to it. A reviewer can be wrong about the work. A confirmer owns the consequence of being wrong. The Kitchen places verification at Gate 6 and the release decision at Gate 7. This repository treats them as one moment with two parts, because the evidence gathered at Gate 6 exists to make the Gate 7 decision knowing.

## The bracket

The two moments bracket everything else. Inside the bracket, work may be delegated to colleagues, to counsel, to vendors and to AI, in bounded ways, with the boundaries set by the definition. Bounded is doing real work in that sentence. The Kitchen still governs the middle: the Manual First Pass is never delegable to the tool, and Gate 4 bounds what AI may touch and when. The bracket is not a license to hollow out the middle. It is the location of the two decisions that cannot be delegated even in part. Outside the bracket there is nothing to delegate, because outside the bracket there is no task yet and no reliance yet.

This is the answer to the recurring question of what remains for the Human when AI produces the work. The Human's job was never primarily production. It was always to decide what the work must do and to stand behind the decision that it does it. Coordination and drafting obscured this. Their removal reveals it.

## Exploratory work is not an exception

The common objection: some tasks cannot be defined in advance because their shape is unknown at the start. Research, investigation, diagnosis and discovery all seem to resist a prior definition of done, and professionals doing that work will be tempted to skip moment one as unrealistic.

The objection confuses the deliverable with the exploration. When the deliverable cannot yet be defined, define done for the exploration itself: the question stated in one sentence, what would count as an answer, the sources that must be consulted before any conclusion is drawn, the stopping rule that ends the digging and the form of the interim output. Every one of those is knowable before the first search. When the exploration hardens into a deliverable, write the deliverable's definition then, as a recorded step with its own moment one. Two definitions in sequence, each preceding its own work, are the honest treatment of exploratory tasks. One definition written after the answer is known is the dishonest one, because it will have been written by the answer.

The bracket also explains why the definition must precede the draft. Once output exists, it argues for its own acceptance. It anchors the Human on the structure it chose, the issues it covered and the standard it happens to meet. A definition written after the draft is a description of the draft. It will pass the draft, because it was reverse-engineered from the draft. The only anchor that can compete with the model's anchor is the one set before the model speaks.

## Amendment, not drift

Definitions of done may change mid-task. They should change, when the manual first pass or the AI-assisted work surfaces something the Human did not know at define time. Learning is not a failure of the method. Unrecorded learning is.

The rule is amendment, not drift. An amendment states what changed, why, on whose authority and on what date, in the amendment log of the definition itself. Amendment authority follows ownership: the Owner amends at Tiers 1 and 2, and at Tier 3 the amendment carries the Approver's sign-off as well. Whoever holds the consequence of the relaxed standard is the only party entitled to relax it. Drift is the same change made silently: scope quietly narrowed, a test quietly weakened, a constraint quietly reclassified as a preference. Drift discovered at confirmation means the work is not done. It means the definition was undone, and by nobody in particular, which is exactly the condition Final Liability exists to prevent.

Amendment discipline has one more benefit. It converts deadline pressure from a corrupting force into a recorded decision. When time runs out and the work does not meet the definition, the Human has three honest options and one dishonest one. The honest options: refuse to confirm, negotiate more time or amend the definition openly and own the residual risk. The dishonest option is to confirm anyway and let the definition mean whatever the deadline required. The amendment log is what makes the honest path administratively cheap enough to take.

## The six false forms of done

Each of these substitutes something observable and easy for the decision that is hard. Each is common. None is a confirmation.

1. **Done by exhaustion.** The time or budget ran out, so the state of the work at that moment was declared final. Effort spent is not a property of the work.
2. **Done by fluency.** It reads well, so it must be right. Fluency is the one property a language model guarantees, which makes it the least informative signal available.
3. **Done by volume.** It is long and has headings for everything, so it must be complete. Coverage of headings is not coverage of substance, and the missing issue has no heading.
4. **Done by deadline.** The date arrived. Dates are properties of calendars.
5. **Done by silence.** It circulated and nobody objected. Silence measures the reviewers' attention and incentives, not the work. An unread document survives circulation perfectly.
6. **Done by the tool.** The model states that it has addressed all requirements. The model is reporting on its own output using the same process that produced the output. Self-attestation by the system under review is evidence of nothing except the system's training.

The six forms share a structure: each replaces the question "does the work meet the definition" with a question that answers itself. The Salience Audit in [02-salient-factors.md](02-salient-factors.md) exists because the replacement is rarely deliberate. It happens because the false signal is salient and the true test is work.

## What this file establishes

Done is a decision made twice by a Human who can be held to it: defined before generation, confirmed before reliance. The definition precedes the draft, changes only by recorded amendment and is confirmed against evidence by a named Human with three lawful outcomes available. The rest of this repository is about why those decisions are harder than they look and how to make them well anyway.
