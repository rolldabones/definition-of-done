# Salient Factors

*Salience against materiality, the four distortions and the Salience Audit*

## The definition

A Salient Factor is whatever commands attention at the moment of decision. Salience is a property of presentation: position, prominence, fluency, repetition, vividness, confidence of register. Materiality is a property of consequence: what changes the outcome for the parties who bear it. These are different dimensions. They correlate weakly in ordinary work and worse in AI-mediated work, because a generation process optimizes exactly the properties that produce salience and is indifferent to the properties that produce materiality.

The work of the Human at both moments of done is to manage the gap. At define time, the risk is writing tests for what is salient about the task rather than what is material about the consequence. At confirm time, the risk is accepting work because its salient properties are excellent while its material defects sit quietly off the page.

Neither risk is a character flaw. Attention is a finite resource allocated by cues, and presentation controls the cues. The decision science literature has documented the machinery for decades: availability, vividness and recency drive judged importance, and what is out of sight is priced at approximately zero. The doctrine here does not ask the Human to be immune. It asks the Human to run a procedure that does not depend on immunity.

## The four distortions of AI-mediated work

Generated output distorts salience in four characteristic ways. Each converts a property of the generator into an apparent property of the work.

**1. Fluency reads as accuracy.** Polish is the cheapest property a language model produces and the strongest cue a reader receives. The Kitchen's facilitator principle states the operational consequence: the most dangerous AI outputs are often the most fluent ones. Fluency is not evidence of anything except fluency.

**2. Coverage reads as completeness.** Generated documents produce headings, sections and enumerations at near-zero cost, so the artifact presents the shape of exhaustiveness. But the missing issue has no heading. Absence is never salient, and generated structure actively suppresses the question of absence by filling the visual field with presence.

**3. Confidence reads as verification.** The register of generated prose is calibrated to sound settled, whether the underlying claim is a verbatim quotation from a statute or an invention. The confidence of the prose and the state of the evidence are independent variables. Treating tone as a proxy for verification is how hallucinated citations pass review.

**4. The offered frame reads as the option space.** Whatever the model surfaces becomes the agenda. Three options presented are read as the three options that exist. Risks listed are read as the risk universe. This distortion is treated in depth in [Order of Elimination](03-order-of-elimination.md) and [Choice Architecture](04-choice-architecture.md), because it governs not only what the Human sees but what the Human can no longer see.

## Where material factors hide

Material but non-salient factors cluster in known places. The list is a hunting map, not a taxonomy.

- **In absences.** The source not cited, the jurisdiction not covered, the stakeholder not consulted, the counterparty obligation without a flow-down, the edge case without a paragraph.
- **In hardened assumptions.** Premises that entered as estimates and now read as facts. The GRC Workbook's internal context discipline names this pattern: assumptions that have hardened into facts.
- **In base rates.** The reference class the vivid case displaced. The Bias Check in the [Risk-Informed Decision Making Prompt](https://github.com/rolldabones/risk-informed-decision-making-prompt) carries this as recallability and base-rate neglect.
- **In distribution tails.** The 95th percentile outcome behind the mean estimate. Treated in [06-averages-variances-uncertainties.md](06-averages-variances-uncertainties.md).
- **In discarded alternatives.** The option eliminated in round one on the information available in round one. Treated in [03-order-of-elimination.md](03-order-of-elimination.md).
- **In the framing itself.** The default, the anchor and the gain-loss presentation that arrived with the draft. Treated in [04-choice-architecture.md](04-choice-architecture.md).
- **In the quiet trade.** The criterion that was subordinated without anyone recording the subordination. Treated in [05-trade-offs.md](05-trade-offs.md).

## The Salience Audit

Run the audit twice: on the task at define time and on the artifact at confirm time. It is seven questions. Answer them in writing. One line each is sufficient for Tier 1 and Tier 2 work under the Kitchen's tiers. Tier 3 work answers all seven with evidence pointers.

1. **The hostile reader.** What would a hostile and competent reviewer look for first? Is there a test for it in the definition?
2. **The quiet giant.** What is the least discussed element with the largest consequence if wrong?
3. **The unnamed party.** Which affected party has not been named in the work or the definition? The Kitchen's Gate 3 question applies: who could be affected if this output is wrong?
4. **The naked assertion.** Which material claim is asserted but not evidenced? Distinguish the confidence of the prose from the state of the record.
5. **The missing heading.** What issue would a specialist expect to find that has no section? Search for what the structure omits, not what it contains.
6. **The inherited agenda.** Every matter arrives already framed by someone: the requester, the counterparty, the draft, the tool. What does that framing make prominent, what does it bury and what did I never consider because it was never presented?
7. **The absent evidence.** What document, number or authority should exist if the work is right, and is it attached?

A definition of done for Tier 2 and above should carry at least one test that could only have come from the audit: a test for an absence, an assumption or a tail rather than for a visible feature of the expected artifact. If every test in the definition describes something the finished document will visibly contain, the definition was written by salience.

## The rule

Salience is data about presentation, not about the world. Use it as evidence of what the presentation rewards. Never use it as evidence of what matters. What matters is established by consequence, and consequence is established by the work of the Human: the digging this repository's remaining lenses exist to structure.
